Buttermilk Biscuits

These simple, easy to make buttermilk biscuits are a great way to compliment a variety of meals; slice in half for a breakfast delight, or slather in butter for a guilty pleasure filled with the essence of home, these biscuits will be the perfect addition to your recipe book!

Many of us have had a wonderful meal with friends or family, perfectly complimented by one of these fluffy pillows of joy and wished we could bring this pleasure back to our homes. But alas, any store-bought sleeve of these doughy discs quickly turns to be a can of disappointment: too dry, too dense, undercooked, burnt, these easy-to-make friends just never seem to come out right.

But I've got a secret for you: it really is that simple!...we just have to take matters into our own hands.

With this super easy-to-make biscuit recipe, your oven will be popping out little buttons of heaven in no time, and all your friends and family will be reminiscing about their delicious meals with you!

Ingredients:

  • 21⁄2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 Tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1⁄2 cup cold butter thinly sliced
  • 1 cup cold buttermilk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt
  3. Add butter and cut in with a pastry blender until pea-size clumps form
  4. Create a well in the center of the mixture by pushing the mixture toward the sides of the bowl. Pour buttermilk into the well. Mix gently with a fork until just combined (over mixing will make tough biscuits!)
  5. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and create a rectangle using your hands
  6. Fold the dough into thirds (like folding a letter: fold left side over center, then right side, so it's still in a rectangular shape). Then, roll back out to 1" thick. Repeat the folding and rolling process twice more, rolling the dough out to 1⁄2" thick the final time
  7. Cut out about 12 biscuits with a 21⁄2"-3" cutter. Re-roll any scraps until there's about 10-12 biscuits
  8. Transfer biscuits to a baking sheet so that the biscuits are touching each other and the edge
  9. Bake at 425° for about 13-15 minutes until cooked through and golden brown on top

Notes:

  • Don't have buttermilk on hand? Try this super useful 2 ingredient buttermilk substitute
  • Make sure baking powder is fresh for the fluffiest biscuits
  • For crispy biscuit edges, brush unbaked tops with 2 Tbsp buttermilk, and cook in a cast iron skillet
  • To prepare in a food processor: Combine dry ingredients in step 2 in your food processor. Pulse to combine. Then add butter. Pulse again until pea-sized crumbs form (you do not want it to turn into a dough yet!). Then transfer mixture to a large mixing bowl and continue with recipe instructions
  • Storage: Cover leftovers tightly, and store at room temperature for up to 5 days
  • Recommended equipment: pastry blender | biscuit cutters
